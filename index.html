Olivia V. J. Levinsen

Welcome to my webpage, and thank you for stopping by!

I am a PhD Fellow at the Department of Political Science, University of Copenhagen. My research focuses on political (under)representation of women and the working class within the ODER (Origins of Descriptive Representation) project, funded by the Carlsberg Foundation and led by Frederik Hjorth. In the project, we use optical character recognition (OCR), computer vision, and political data science to digitize all candidacies and candidate-level election results across Denmark's democratic history. This comprehensive dataset allows us to study how candidates' socioeconomic backgrounds have evolved over time and what this means for political representation and public policy. I am responsible for the ODER track on women's representation. My PhD has a historical focus on barriers and obstacles to political representation in Denmark from the Constitution in 1849 to today.

I studied political science at Aarhus University, completing both my bachelor's and master's degrees. During my master's, I was a visiting student at Sciences Po Bordeaux, which build on a longer spark for France and French culture; I have spent many summers there and worked for a year as a research assistant.

My current research examines political representation broadly, with a particular focus on women's political representation. Topics I work on include parties' nomination of women candidates, party members' political ambition over time, women leaders and cabinet appointments, and social movements' ability to increase political representation. I am also interested in descriptive, subjective, and symbolic representation and how different groups' interests are translated into policy.

I live at Østerbro in Copenhagen with my fiancé Anders who has the worst jokes in the world. However, I'll never forget my roots from Aarhus and will never stop talking about how much this city has to offer.
